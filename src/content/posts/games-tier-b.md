## Alba - A Wildlife Adventure

**Type:**

> Casual, Wholesome, Family Friendly

**Description:**

> Even the smallest person can make a big difference. Join Alba, as she sets out to save her beautiful island and its wildlife. And possibly start a revolution.

**Quick Review:**
> What a simple, great experience.  
> Notice I'm not saying game, because as game - in the sense of playing - this is barely more then a walking simulator with VERY little of Pokemon Snap like ideas.  
>   
> Don't get me wrong, the art design, sound design and general world building and atmosphere is TOP notch, the gameplay itself is on the downside, but that is not a killer feature here.  
>   
> It's a very wholesome story / experience that you can play with your significant other watching - or the other way around -, or give it to your kid, which is even better.  
>   
> Rather short, about 3 - 4 hours to 100% the game, and the game has 0 replayability, so take that into consideration when calculating your € / hour.  
>   
> Still, an easy recommendation if you enjoy a relaxed, lay back game, where you can just enjoy the atmosphere and world building and not having to worry about QTE, builds, progression and such stuff.

## Breamble: The Mountain King

**Type:**

> Adventure, Cinematic, Atmospheric

**Description:**

> Bramble The Mountain King is a grim adventure set in a world inspired by dark, Nordic fables. Explore the beautiful yet dangerous and twisted land of Bramble in your endeavour to rescue your sister. Traverse a wondrous landscape and survive deadly encounters with Bramble's many hideous creatures.

**Quick Review:**
> A great visual experience. Sound design is very good as well.  
> As a game, it's lackluster.  
> Way too simple in both mechanics and interactivity, you have to follow it's rules exactly, absolutely no way to change or influence anything or play differently.  
>   
> This one I would seriously only recommend it if you either a) get a steep discount - think 6 hours, at best, from start to finish - b) Swedish children stories mean a lot to you or c) you can be entertained with good photography design and pretty graphics, and don't mind much a lackluster gameplay, even better, you enjoy it being lackluster and rather on the "simple" side.  
>   
> Still recommend it, the art and sound design are just very high quality, but keep your expectations in check / be mindful of your own tastes and priorities about gameplay.  
>   
> A Solid B. (so above average, good game)

## Carrion

> Villain Protagonist, Action, Metroidvania

## Destroy All Humans

**Type:**

> Villain Protagonist, Action, Third Person, Sci-fi, Remake

**Description:**

> The cult-classic returns! Terrorize the people of 1950's Earth in the role of the evil alien Crypto-137. Harvest their DNA and bring down the US government in the faithful remake of the legendary alien invasion action adventure. Annihilate puny humans using an assortment of alien weaponry and psychic abilities.

**Quick Review:**

## RoboCop: Rogue City

**Type:**

> FPS, Action, Shooter, RPG, Cyberpunk

**Description:**

> Become the legendary part man, part machine, all cop hero and deliver justice in Old Detroit.

**Quick Review:**
> This is an easy recommendation.  
> Now, depending if you enjoyed RoboCop the Movie and have "nostalgia" or whatever you want to call it, this will be either a "good game" or an "above average".  
>   
> if you have nostalgia for the Movie and what not, then this might even be a "very good game": a lot of easter eggs and movie references, amazing sound design, original RoboCop Voice Actor, top notch violence, the gameplay feels like you are actually playing RoboCop.  
>   
> If you don't have nostalgia, the above still applies, as well as all the quick inserted ethical and moral questions - which are just 1 or 2 sentences and then never touched again -, the 80-90s humor, the dystopian visions and ideas about a "unchecked capitalism" and so on, BUT it might not be enough to make you enjoy the game that much.  
>   
> If you saw the movie, this will feel "just like home", if not, you might linger for more development, more character development, more exposition and exploration of said ideas and feel like some dialogues, quests and situations are just badly done - which don't get me wrong, they are, but they feel just like the type of humor and delivery from the movie period -.  
>   
> With that said, the shooting / gameplay mechanics are not anything special, nothing pratically new, so as a FPS, the game is just very lacking. Same in the other departments, be it "detective" or any other "puzzle" like elements.  
>   
> The only major downside of the game, imho, is the lack of manual saves, you are bond to the - sometimes very much apart from one another - checkpoint system.  
>   
> About 20 hours for a run while checking every nook and spot, about 8 hours for the main story only.  
>   
> So, an easy recommendation, but this game will either be "an amazing homage to the original" or "an average FPS" depending on how much other media knowledge and "nostalgia" you have.

## Trek to Yomi

**Type:**

> Hack and Slash, 2D, Swordplay, Combat

**Description:**

> As a vow to his dying Master, the young swordsman Hiroki is sworn to protect his town and the people he loves against all threats. Faced with tragedy and bound to duty, the lone samurai must voyage beyond life and death to confront himself and decide his path forward.

**Quick Review:**
> This is one of those games that while I will recommend, this is not a recommendation for everyone, and you should really consider the price for what you getting.  
>   
> So, the game is visually amazing. The story itself is nice and the "multiple endings" add a bit of variety to it but not that much. But boy, is the combat system repetitive.  
> Once you unlock the finisher of Up + Light - Light - Light - Heavy you have seen everything there is to see for your own worthfull skills.  
> The enemies don't have the same rules of engagement as you, so between reloading canon shots in sub 2 seconds or infinite combos, it can become frustrating from time to time.  
>   
> Doing 100% achievements isn't "ideal" per say, as in, for a few achievements you basically have one shot / no way to go back to a previous chapter, so you might as well just save scum when going for 100%, specifically in 2-3 achievements.  
>   
> Expect about 4-6 hours for 1 playthough, 8-12 hours for 100%.  
> All in all, still very recommended much recommended if you buy it for a good price due to the visuals alone.
